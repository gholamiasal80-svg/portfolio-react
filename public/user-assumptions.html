User Assumptions
Understanding potential
user needs

This project didn't include formal user research or interviews. These assumptions are based on a general understanding of people who might use a mental health app. They helped guide early design decisions and kept the focus on the user throughout the process.

😟
Feeling Overwhelmed

Users often feel anxious when first reaching out for mental health support, so the app needs to feel calm and non-intimidating from the start.

🔍
Quick Discovery

Users want to find the right psychologist quickly without going through a long or complicated process.

👤
Need to Trust First

Users prefer seeing a psychologist's specialty and a short bio before making any decision to book a session.

📱
Comfort Over Camera

Users are more likely to follow through with a session if they can choose between video and phone — not everyone is comfortable on camera.

✅
Reassurance Matters

Users want a clear booking confirmation so they feel certain their session is actually scheduled and won't be missed.

⚡
Low Friction Moments

Users may be opening the app during stressful moments, so every step needs to be simple with as few taps as possible.

Assumptions are not validated — they represent a starting point for further research.
